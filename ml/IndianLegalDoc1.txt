Articles 150 to 160 of the Indian Constitution pertain to various aspects of governmental accounting and the executive branch at the state level. Here's a concise overview:

Article 150: Form of Accounts of the Union and the States This article mandates that the accounts of both the Union and State governments be maintained in a format prescribed by the President, based on the advice of the Comptroller and Auditor-General (CAG) of India.

Article 151: Audit Reports It requires that the reports of the CAG, concerning the accounts of the Union and States, be submitted to the President or the respective Governors. These reports are then presented before the Parliament or the State Legislatures.

Article 152: Definition This article defines the term "State" for the purposes of Part VI of the Constitution, excluding the state of Jammu and Kashmir.

Article 153: Governors of States It stipulates that there shall be a Governor for each state in India.

Article 154: Executive Power of State The executive power of the state is vested in the Governor and is exercised by them either directly or through officers subordinate to them, in accordance with the Constitution.

Article 155: Appointment of Governor The Governor of a state is appointed by the President of India by warrant under his hand and seal.

Article 156: Term of Office of Governor The Governor holds office during the pleasure of the President. The normal term is five years, but it can be terminated earlier.

Article 157: Qualifications for Appointment as Governor A person must be a citizen of India and at least 35 years old to be eligible for appointment as a Governor.

Article 158: Conditions of Governor's Office This article outlines the conditions of the Governor's office, including prohibitions on holding other offices of profit and entitlements to emoluments and allowances.

Article 159: Oath or Affirmation by the Governor Before assuming office, the Governor must take an oath or affirmation to faithfully execute the office and preserve, protect, and defend the Constitution and the law.

Article 160: Discharge of the Functions of the Governor in Certain Contingencies In situations not provided for in the Constitution, the President can make provisions for the discharge of the functions of the Governor of a state.

These articles collectively establish the framework for financial accounting and the executive authority at the state level in India.
